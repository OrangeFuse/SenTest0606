The SENCO bottleneck

Inclusion cannot sit with one person.

The source site is clear about the central tension: SENCOs are carrying too much responsibility alone. The better answer is shared practice with strong SENCO oversight.

Reduce manual chasing

Actions, reviews and evidence become easier to see without relying on constant follow-up.

Strengthen quality assurance

A shared workflow gives SENCOs a clearer view of consistency, gaps and support needs.

Protect strategic capacity

Time released from administration can be redirected towards leadership, training, review and provision planning.

SENCO FAQs

Position Embrace as infrastructure, not another admin tool.

Is Embrace just another provision map?

No. Provision maps can be one output, but the core idea is a wider inclusion operating system for practice, evidence and oversight.

Will it replace the SENCO role?

No. The proposition is the opposite: reducing firefighting so the SENCO role can become more strategic.

Does it reduce admin?

SENshine’s current estimate is a saving of around 46 days per average school per year through streamlined planning, evidence-gathering and reporting.

Can I see what is happening across the school?

Yes. Embrace is designed to give visibility over actions, interventions, consistency and impact without chasing staff manually.

The teacher problem

Confidence varies when support depends on who knows what, and when.

Teachers are expected to identify need early, adapt practice and evidence what has happened. The problem is that specialist advice is often delayed, inconsistent or locked inside the SENCO’s head.

What changes

  • Clearer actions to try in class.
  • Evidence-informed guidance without waiting months for external input.
  • Structured records created as part of everyday support, not as extra paperwork.
  • Professional judgement preserved rather than overridden.

Practice first. Any documentation is a by-product of good classroom action, not an extra task.

Teacher FAQs

Answer the objections that block adoption.

Do I need SEND expertise to use Embrace?

No. Embrace is designed to support teachers at the point of need with expert-reviewed guidance that builds confidence without requiring specialist knowledge.

Will this add to my workload?

The intended direction is the opposite: turn classroom actions into structured, reusable evidence, reducing duplication and repeated rewriting.

Can I still teach in my own style?

Yes. Embrace supports professional judgement. It is not designed to dictate a single teaching style.

What if I am worried about getting SEND wrong?

The platform gives evidence-informed reassurance and helps staff act earlier rather than waiting for external advice.
